# Luma Village: seven-day launch playbook

Live game: https://false-start.vercel.app/

Share challenge: https://false-start.vercel.app/?challenge=market

## What we are testing

This is not a big launch. It is a seven-day demand test for one promise:

> Buy breakfast in Spanish in 60 seconds, then keep learning inside a cozy world.

The first goal is 25 real players who form an opinion. We care more about
completed Market Day conversations and honest return intent than likes or raw
traffic.

The initial audience is narrow on purpose: English-speaking adult beginners who
want useful Spanish and also enjoy cozy games, farming games, or old-school skill
progression.

Keep the first chapter free. Do not add a payment gate until people finish the
challenge, enter the valley, and ask for another region.

## Seven-day sequence

### Day 1: people who know you

Post the personal copy below on the one network where you already have real
relationships. Then send the DM to five people who either learn Spanish or like
cozy games.

Ask for a two-minute test, not support. Make only urgent fixes today.

### Day 2: show the useful win

Post one 18-25 second vertical clip to any accounts you already use. The market
conversation must be visible in the first second. Reuse the recording on Reels,
TikTok, and Shorts only if those accounts already exist.

Reply to comments with:

> What real-life Spanish situation should become the next playable challenge?

### Day 3: one relevant Reddit community

Read the current rules first. Choose one community that permits prototypes or
feedback requests. Disclose that you built it. Do not cross-post the same pitch
to several subreddits.

### Day 4: three small communities

Send the moderator message below to three relevant language-learning, cozy-game,
indie-game, teacher, or local conversation communities. Ask permission before
posting a link.

### Day 5: Show HN

Use the supplied Show HN copy. Post only when you can answer comments for the
next few hours. Be candid that this is an authored, single-player vertical slice.

### Day 6: Product Hunt or a second clip

Use Product Hunt only if the phone experience is reliable after the first five
days. Otherwise skip it and publish a second clip answering the most common
question from testers.

Ask existing testers for feedback, never coordinated upvotes.

### Day 7: publish the numbers

Share visits, challenge starts, challenge finishes, valley entries, and the
biggest abandonment point. Pick exactly one next build: activation, retention,
or a new practical situation.

## What to measure

Vercel Web Analytics measures visits and referrers on all plans after it is
enabled. The app also sends named funnel events; Vercel currently exposes custom
event reporting on paid plans. If your project is on Hobby, manually count the
first 25 testers and use their answers instead of upgrading just for this test.

| Metric | First-week interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| Qualified visitors | Get at least 25 before drawing a conclusion. |
| Challenge starts / visitors | Under 50% means the promise or first screen is weak. |
| First responses / starts | Under 85% means the interaction is unclear. |
| Challenge completions / starts | 60%+ is a promising opening; under 40% needs repair. |
| Valley entries / completions | 50%+ means the practical win creates curiosity. |
| First quest completions / valley entries | Under 50% means world onboarding is the next fix. |
| Share clicks / completions | Any 10%+ signal is useful this early. |
| "Felt useful" votes | Look for 70%+ yes and read every "not yet" explanation. |
| Next-day returns | Ten of the first 50 genuine players returning would justify a retention build. |
| Region-two demand | Ten specific requests matter more than a large number of likes. |

The instrumented event names are:

- `challenge_started`
- `first_response`
- `hint_used`
- `challenge_completed`
- `challenge_feedback`
- `entered_world`
- `quest_completed`
- `chapter_completed`
- `share_clicked`

After seven days:

- Continue if people finish, enter the valley, and several return or request
  another practical situation.
- Fix activation if people like the idea but abandon Market Day or the first
  village quest.
- Reposition or stop expanding if visits produce compliments but almost no
  completed conversations, returns, recall, or region-two demand.

## Do not spend money yet

- No paid social ads.
- No influencer sponsorships.
- No launch agency, upvote package, promotional bot, or PR service.
- No native app solely for distribution.
- No multiplayer servers or open-ended AI conversation bill.
- No giant content library.
- No payment infrastructure until players ask for more.
- No brand redesign before the opening loop earns retention.

The first sensible spend is a short memorable domain after people return. The
second is art or audio help for a practical scenario that testers specifically
request.

## Ask every serious tester

1. Without me explaining it, what did you think you were supposed to do first?
2. What was the first moment you felt confused, impatient, or tempted to leave?
3. Which real-life Spanish situation would make you return for chapter two?

Then ask separately:

> If new practical regions preserved your character and skills, would you pay
> $12 per year? What would have to be true for that to feel worth it?

Write down their exact wording. The phrase repeated by people who finish and
return should shape the next landing page.
